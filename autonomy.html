autonomy

Every day, the prospect of a happy, fulfilling life seems increasingly distant. All that's left for us is a future where nothing is guaranteed—not happiness, nor stability, nor comfort. Our very lives aren’t guaranteed in a system built to screw over all but a “lucky” few.

How do they do it? Our parents, our grandparents, our professors, our fellow students, even?

Do what?

Keep on living, keep on pushing forward, no matter how tired you get.

Well, it’s not like we have much of a choice. We can’t cut ourselves out of this system. To live is to participate, whether we like it or not. But, though we may be compelled to keep running and running with our eyes trained on the imaginary finish line, that doesn’t mean we must surrender ourselves completely to the never-ending race.

Yes, we cannot afford to stop moving forward. But that doesn’t mean we cannot still slow down, take time to breathe, and express care and love for our fellow human beings.

Love?

commodity

The world treats us as products with no inherent value. That makes us all disposable. To avoid such a fate, we must constantly optimize. Level up, like a video game character. Gain skills, gain stats, gain connections.

But neither of us blindly believe that, do we? The value of a human being is not measured by statistics. We don’t choose our friends based on their financial status, or their GPA, or how many languages they can speak, or how many awards they’ve won. When our friends show weakness, we don’t abandon them and replace them with someone "better", like the system would do to us.

Is that not the perfect counter to the commodification of self?

Sure, we all know that, but does that change anything?

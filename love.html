love

Do you remember a time when the future didn’t seem so terrifying? Remember when you were young, and you didn’t fully understand what it meant to grow up, but you knew that it meant freedom? You knew that it meant understanding more about the world, more about yourself, and being able to choose what you wanted to do. And, in that innocent view of the future, one thing remained constant—the presence of a loving family and caring friends.

It was a future surrounded by love.

Why shouldn’t that future become a reality? Can we not still choose to love and care for one another just for the sake of it? In a world that pushes individualism and self-interest, the choice to love one another is a display of strength. Even if it slows us down, even if it means risking criticism and ridicule, even if it means expressing vulnerability and weakness in a world that punishes such qualities.

To choose to love, again and again.

And again.

And again.

Together, carving out small pockets of resistance through collective care and collective hope. Sharing the burden of precarity and risk, creating our own stability. We can’t build a new world in one day. Or one year. Or ten, even. But change happens. The seeds are there. And when they are nurtured by the hands of mutual care, the roots that grow between the cracks have the power to undermine the hegemony of fear.

So, do you still fear the future?
